# Decision-letter review and options

## Extract before interpreting

Record decision date; issues decided; favorable findings; service-connection elements; diagnostic codes/evaluations/effective dates; evidence list; reasons; duty-to-assist findings; examination reliance; review options and stated deadlines. Preserve the exact letter language and page citations.

## Analyze each issue separately

Identify what was granted, denied, deferred, reduced, or remanded; the element VA found missing; contrary evidence; unresolved favorable findings; rating/effective-date basis; and any factual or legal error. Do not assume a boilerplate phrase controls over the complete reasons and evidence.

## Review lanes

Supplemental Claim focuses on new and relevant evidence; Higher-Level Review generally reexamines the existing record and can include an informal conference but no new evidence; Board lanes differ by evidence and hearing choices. Use the current official pages and the actual notice. [VA-SUPPLEMENTAL] [VA-HLR] [VA-BOARD] [CFR-3.2500]

Remands require completion of ordered development; substantial noncompliance can be material. [CASE-STEGALL]

CUE, reduction, severance, court appeal, and deadline problems are high-risk legal issues. Provide education and refer promptly to accredited counsel/representation.
