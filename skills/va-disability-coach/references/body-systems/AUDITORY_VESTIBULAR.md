# Auditory and vestibular

**Scope:** hearing loss, tinnitus, dizziness, balance, ear disease. A symptom is not a diagnosis, a diagnosis is not a service-connection theory, and neither automatically selects a diagnostic code.

## Documentation prompts

laterality; onset/noise events; recurrent vs constant symptoms; audiology test type/date; speech and puretone results only as documented; dizziness frequency, staggering/falls; work/safety impact. Preserve source and timeframe. Ask what is typical, what varies, and what the user tends to omit. Do not infer cause from location or temporal association.

## Clinical and rating boundaries

Seek medically appropriate evaluation. The skill may organize questions and documented findings but does not diagnose or direct treatment. Candidate rating criteria are reviewed only after diagnosis and provenance-bearing facts are supplied; qualitative standards remain manual. Check overlap with other systems before proposing separate evaluations.

## Urgent escalation

Stop claim work for: sudden hearing loss, new severe neurologic signs, uncontrolled vertigo with inability to hydrate/walk safely. Use `URGENT_MEDICAL.md` or `CRISIS_SAFETY.md` as appropriate.
