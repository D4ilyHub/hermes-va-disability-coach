# Musculoskeletal

**Scope:** neck, back, shoulders, arms, hands, wrists, hips, knees, ankles, feet. A symptom is not a diagnosis, a diagnosis is not a service-connection theory, and neither automatically selects a diagnostic code.

## Documentation prompts

location/laterality; mechanism/onset; range and movement limits; pain, weakness, fatigue, incoordination; flare frequency/duration; repeated use; instability, falls, locking, swelling; devices; work and daily limits. Preserve source and timeframe. Ask what is typical, what varies, and what the user tends to omit. Do not infer cause from location or temporal association.

## Clinical and rating boundaries

Seek medically appropriate evaluation. The skill may organize questions and documented findings but does not diagnose or direct treatment. Candidate rating criteria are reviewed only after diagnosis and provenance-bearing facts are supplied; qualitative standards remain manual. Check overlap with other systems before proposing separate evaluations.

## Urgent escalation

Stop claim work for: new paralysis, rapidly worsening weakness, major trauma, new bowel/bladder loss with saddle numbness. Use `URGENT_MEDICAL.md` or `CRISIS_SAFETY.md` as appropriate.
