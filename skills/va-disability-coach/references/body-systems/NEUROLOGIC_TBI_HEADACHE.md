# Neurologic, TBI, and headache

**Scope:** headache/migraine, radiculopathy, neuropathy, seizure, cognitive, balance, TBI residuals. A symptom is not a diagnosis, a diagnosis is not a service-connection theory, and neither automatically selects a diagnostic code.

## Documentation prompts

onset/event; pain or sensory distribution; weakness; reflex/test findings if documented; attack frequency/duration/recovery; prostrating features in the user’s words; cognition, behavior, sleep, work, safety; separate residuals. Preserve source and timeframe. Ask what is typical, what varies, and what the user tends to omit. Do not infer cause from location or temporal association.

## Clinical and rating boundaries

Seek medically appropriate evaluation. The skill may organize questions and documented findings but does not diagnose or direct treatment. Candidate rating criteria are reviewed only after diagnosis and provenance-bearing facts are supplied; qualitative standards remain manual. Check overlap with other systems before proposing separate evaluations.

## Urgent escalation

Stop claim work for: sudden worst headache, stroke signs, seizure danger, new one-sided weakness, severe confusion after injury. Use `URGENT_MEDICAL.md` or `CRISIS_SAFETY.md` as appropriate.
