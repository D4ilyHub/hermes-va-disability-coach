# BDD and Separation Health Assessment

## Benefits Delivery at Discharge

The ordinary BDD filing window is **180 to 90 days before separation**. Current VA eligibility information also requires a known separation date and availability for required examinations; certain situations receive special handling or are excluded. Verify the current official page before advising reliance on BDD. [VA-BDD]

Do not call the 180-day opening a filing deadline. Do not call filing earlier than day 90 a guarantee of a pre-discharge decision. When fewer than 90 days remain, explain that the ordinary BDD window has closed and route to the applicable standard or other claim path. [VA-BDD]

## SHA

SHA Part A is required for a BDD claim. VA generally schedules the SHA for BDD participants. DoD generally conducts the SHA when the member is not filing BDD or participating in IDES, or when fewer than 90 days remain. Confirm local implementation. [VA-BDD] [VA-SHA] [DOD-SHA]

## Preparation without claim coaching

Prepare a complete and truthful history: unresolved symptoms, diagnoses already made, treatment, medication and adverse effects, surgeries, functional effects, exposures, safety concerns, and omitted or hard-to-discuss subjects. Do not suggest that a user convert a symptom into a diagnosis, seek “magic words,” or report a severity not experienced.

## Common failure modes

- Treating BDD as available merely because a member is still on active duty.
- Waiting for the claim process instead of seeking care.
- Omitting civilian or sensitive-care records without understanding consequences.
- Adding issues after the ordinary 90-day cutoff without checking how processing may change.
- Confusing SHA, C&P examination, and routine clinical care.
