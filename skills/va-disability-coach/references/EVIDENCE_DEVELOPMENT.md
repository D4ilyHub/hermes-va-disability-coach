# Evidence development and gap analysis

## Evidence types

Organize service/personnel records, medical records, diagnostic studies, prescriptions, work or school records, lay statements, line-of-duty findings, exposure evidence, C&P/DBQ reports, and decisions. State what each item proves—or does not prove. [VA-EVIDENCE]

## Gap categories

- **Existence:** no current diagnosis or insufficient evidence of persistent disability.
- **Service event/status:** missing event, duty status, location, line-of-duty, or exposure evidence.
- **Relationship:** no competent nexus evidence, inadequate rationale, or unaddressed alternative cause.
- **Severity:** missing frequency, objective findings, functional loss, treatment requirements, or timeframe.
- **Procedure:** missing notice, records request, favorable finding, examination report, or deadline.
- **Conflict:** inconsistent onset, diagnosis, severity, or service history.

For each gap, list a truthful development option and who controls it. Never manufacture a retrospective detail. Lay statements should identify personal knowledge, concrete examples, approximate dates, and uncertainty. [FORM-21-10210] [CASE-LAYNO]

## Examination adequacy

Potential concerns include inaccurate facts, unanswered questions, lack of rationale, ignored lay reports, failure to address flare-ups or repeated use, or missing required testing. Adequacy is a legal/medical question; preserve the report and seek accredited review. [CASE-BARR] [CASE-NIEVES] [CASE-CORREIA] [CASE-SHARP]
