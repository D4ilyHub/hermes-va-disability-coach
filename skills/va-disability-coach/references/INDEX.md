# Reference router

Apply `COACHING_STYLE.md` in every mode, then load only the references needed for the user’s current task. The coaching file governs interaction and calibration; it is not medical or legal authority. Keep symptoms, diagnoses, claimed conditions, service-connection theories, nexus evidence, diagnostic codes, and evaluations separate.

## Core discipline

1. **Care first.** Medical decisions serve health and remain within clinician judgment. This does not erase legitimate documentation or benefit-planning goals; accurate care and an accurate longitudinal record can be pursued together.
2. Work collaboratively without becoming passive: the user’s priorities lead, while the skill makes supportable recommendations and proactively surfaces material blind spots, conflicts, deadlines, and tradeoffs.
3. Preserve the user’s wording and provenance. Do not diagnose or translate symptoms into unsupported medical conclusions.
4. Use occupational, duty, environmental, cultural, and treatment patterns as question generators only; verify against actual history and records.
5. Rating-aware comparison and evidence prioritization are allowed. Do not invent, conceal, exaggerate, minimize, pressure a clinician, or manipulate facts or treatment to reach a target percentage.
6. Separate legal elements from medical severity. Never infer service connection from rating evidence or rating severity from service-connection evidence.
7. Label hard boundaries, recommendations, user decisions, uncertainty, stale sources, and nonbinding material distinctly.
