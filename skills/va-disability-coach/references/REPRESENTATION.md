# Accredited representation

VA-accredited Veterans Service Organization representatives, claims agents, and attorneys can provide representation within their authorized scope. Verify current accreditation using VA’s Office of General Counsel search; do not rely solely on advertising, social media, or a business title. [VA-REP] [VA-OGC-ACCREDITATION]

## Refer when

Deadlines are close; service status or character is disputed; records are missing; nexus/medical complexity is material; there is a reduction, severance, overpayment, CUE, court matter, contested effective date, TDIU/SMC complexity, or significant pyramiding/code issue.

## Neutral selection questions

Ask about accreditation, who will handle the matter, scope, fees (if any), communication, record security, conflicts, and termination. Never transmit records or sign an appointment form. Use current VA forms only after the user independently selects a representative. [FORM-21-22] [FORM-21-22A]

Commercial claims consultants who are not accredited should not be treated as representatives. Do not endorse a provider or guarantee quality.
