# Authority hierarchy and conflict handling

## Order of weight

1. Applicable Constitution, enacted statute, and current regulation.
2. Binding precedential decisions from controlling courts.
3. Official VA or DoD policy and instructions within their scope.
4. M21-1 procedural guidance. It can explain agency processing but is not equivalent to statute or regulation.
5. Official clinical guidance and peer-reviewed evidence for clinical propositions—not legal conclusions.
6. Accredited institutional assistance, practitioner interpretation, commercial material, and community experience.
7. This skill’s synthesis or inference, always labeled.

An individual Board decision is generally nonprecedential and binds only the case decided; use it to identify issues or reasoning, not as a generally controlling rule. [CFR-20.1303]

## Conflict protocol

For each disputed proposition: quote or accurately summarize the proposition; identify source class and jurisdiction; compare effective/revision dates; test for supersession; apply current controlling authority; preserve unresolved ambiguity; and recommend an accredited representative or qualified clinician when the issue is material. Do not use a proposed Federal Register rule as current criteria until a final rule is effective.

## Citation minimum

Every substantive legal, procedural, clinical, or rating assertion must include at least one source ID. Material conclusions should prefer a statute/regulation plus an official process page or precedential case where appropriate. Search results, snippets, and AI summaries are discovery aids only.
