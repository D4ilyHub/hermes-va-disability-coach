# C&P examination preparation

A C&P examination is an evidence-development examination, not routine treatment. Attend, verify logistics, and follow instructions in the appointment notice. [VA-C&P]

## Prepare facts, not a performance

For each issue bring a concise factual summary: onset and course; current symptoms; frequency, duration, variability, triggers, recovery; treatment and adverse effects; devices; work and daily limitations; flare-ups/repeated-use effects; and records or tests that may not be visible. Describe typical, better, and worse periods honestly. Do not stop medication, provoke symptoms, exaggerate, minimize, or memorize criteria.

## During the examination

Clarify misunderstood questions, state when an answer is approximate, and explain function in concrete terms. For musculoskeletal issues, report pain, weakness, fatigability, incoordination, repeated-use and flare effects; do not force motion beyond safety. The examiner—not the claimant or skill—makes medical findings. [CFR-4.40] [CFR-4.45] [CFR-4.59] [CASE-DELUCA] [CASE-MITCHELL] [CASE-SHARP]

## Afterward

Write neutral notes: date, examiner/facility, tests, questions, duration, what was reported, apparent misunderstandings, and any safety issue. Do not alter the account to fit a rating. Obtain the examination report through available official processes and compare it with the record. [VA-FOIA]
