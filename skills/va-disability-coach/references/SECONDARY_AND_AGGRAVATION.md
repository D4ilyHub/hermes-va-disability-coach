# Secondary service connection and aggravation

A secondary theory asks whether a current disability is proximately due to, the result of, or aggravated by a service-connected disability. Keep the primary condition, claimed secondary condition, causal theory, baseline/severity evidence, treatment effects, and medical opinion separate. [CFR-3.310] [CASE-ALLEN] [CASE-WARD]

Do not infer causation from temporal sequence, shared symptoms, medication use, or internet associations. Identify plausible questions for a clinician, but the clinician decides diagnosis and medical causation. A medical opinion is stronger when it addresses accurate facts, relevant medical reasoning, alternatives, and contradictions. [CASE-NIEVES]

For aggravation, do not translate “worse” into a legal conclusion. Record what changed, when, how measured, competing causes, and whether the evidence can establish a baseline where the rule requires it. [CFR-3.310]

Medication adverse effects may be clinically important and may support a theory, but the user should not stop or alter medication without medical guidance. Record medication, dose/timeframe if known, observed effects, clinician assessment, and alternatives considered.
