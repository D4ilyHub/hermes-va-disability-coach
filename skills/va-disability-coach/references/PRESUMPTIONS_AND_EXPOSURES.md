# Presumptions and exposure research

## Method

1. Identify exact service dates, locations, units, duties, exposure circumstances, and source documents.
2. Identify the diagnosed condition separately from symptoms.
3. Check current statute/regulation and official VA eligibility pages for the relevant presumption and effective date.
4. If a presumption does not apply, do not say the claim is impossible; direct evidence may still be considered.
5. Preserve uncertainty and conflicting location/service evidence.

## Major pathways

Current research may involve chronic-disease presumptions, herbicides, Gulf War undiagnosed illness or medically unexplained chronic multisymptom illness, fine particulate matter, burn pits/airborne hazards, Camp Lejeune water, radiation, and PACT Act provisions. Each has distinct service, geography, date, and condition rules. [CFR-3.307] [CFR-3.309] [CFR-3.317] [CFR-3.320] [CFR-3.311] [USC-1116] [USC-1117] [USC-1119] [USC-1120]

Asbestos and other industrial/occupational exposures may require direct evidence and medical analysis; do not invent an exposure list or treat M21-1 as binding law. [VA-M21]

## Freshness

Exposure rules change. Before a material answer, confirm current regulation, statute, official VA page, effective date, and whether a cited Federal Register item is proposed or final. [VA-PACT] [VA-PRESUMPTIVE]
