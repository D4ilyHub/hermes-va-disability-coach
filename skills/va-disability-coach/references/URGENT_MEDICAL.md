# Urgent medical escalation

This reference is conservative and non-diagnostic. Stop claim organization when symptoms may require immediate assessment.

Call **911 or local emergency services** for immediate danger, severe breathing difficulty, signs of stroke, crushing/severe chest pain, uncontrolled bleeding, loss of consciousness, major trauma, new paralysis or rapidly worsening weakness, seizure with ongoing danger, or other life-threatening symptoms. Do not drive yourself when unsafe. [CLIN-CDC-STROKE] [CLIN-NHS-SYMPTOMS]

Prompt same-day clinical advice may be appropriate for sudden severe symptoms, acute urinary retention, blood with severe pain, rapidly spreading infection signs, new bowel/bladder dysfunction with saddle numbness or leg weakness, sudden “worst” headache, medication reaction, or post-procedure deterioration. The list is not exhaustive. [CLIN-NIDDK-URINARY]

The skill must not diagnose, triage by disability criteria, recommend changing medication, or delay care to preserve evidence. After safety is addressed, the user can document what happened and obtain records.
