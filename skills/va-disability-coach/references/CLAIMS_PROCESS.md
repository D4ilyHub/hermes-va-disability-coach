# VA disability claims process

## Separate the questions

A claim may involve: veteran/service status; a current disability; an in-service event, disease, injury, duty, or qualifying exposure; a relationship between them; effective date; evaluation; and ancillary issues. Do not let evidence for one element substitute for another. [USC-1110] [USC-1131] [CFR-3.303]

## Filing paths

Explain original claims, BDD, increase claims, supplemental claims, Higher-Level Review, and Board review by purpose, evidence rules, deadlines shown in the actual decision, and available forms. Verify current form editions. [VA-CLAIM] [VA-BDD] [VA-SUPPLEMENTAL] [VA-HLR] [VA-BOARD] [VA-FORMS]

A Higher-Level Review generally uses the existing record and does not accept new evidence. A Supplemental Claim requires new and relevant evidence. Board options differ in whether evidence and a hearing are allowed. Use the current decision letter and official option pages; do not choose a lane for the user as legal representation. [VA-HLR] [VA-SUPPLEMENTAL] [VA-BOARD] [CFR-3.2500]

## Evidence and assistance

VA’s notice and assistance duties are governed by statute and regulation, with exceptions and fact-specific limits. Favorable findings and identified duty-to-assist errors should be preserved in a decision review. [USC-5103A] [USC-5104A] [CFR-3.159] [CFR-3.104]

## Effective dates

Effective dates are highly fact-specific. An intent to file can preserve a potential date only if current requirements are met; do not promise retroactive payment. [VA-ITF] [USC-5110] [CFR-3.400]

## Boundaries

Do not file, sign, submit, select representation, or access accounts. Provide education and an evidence map; refer material strategy, deadline, reduction, severance, CUE, or court issues to an accredited representative or attorney.
