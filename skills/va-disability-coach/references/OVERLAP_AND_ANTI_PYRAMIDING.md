# Overlap and anti-pyramiding

VA generally avoids evaluating the same disability manifestation under multiple diagnoses. Distinct manifestations may sometimes receive separate evaluations, but labels alone do not establish distinct impairment. [CFR-4.14] [CASE-ESTEBAN]

## High-risk patterns

- Multiple mental-health diagnoses using the same occupational/social impairment.
- TBI cognitive, emotional/behavioral, headache, vestibular, or neurologic residuals overlapping another code.
- Spine pain/limited motion plus radiculopathy or other objective neurologic abnormality.
- Scar pain/instability, muscle injury, nerve impairment, and joint limitation from one injury.
- Sleep, fatigue, concentration, and mood symptoms attributed to multiple conditions.
- Headache symptoms under TBI and migraine codes.

Create a manifestation map: manifestation; body function; source; frequency/severity; code using it; whether another code uses the same manifestation; and why separate evaluation may or may not be supportable. If medical evidence cannot separate effects, do not invent attribution. [CASE-MITTLEIDER]

Multiple diagnostic codes may be plausible without all being separately payable. Refer material overlap questions to an accredited representative.
