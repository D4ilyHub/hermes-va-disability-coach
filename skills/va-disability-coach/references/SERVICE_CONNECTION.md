# Service-connection evidence mapping

## Direct theory

Map evidence separately to: current disability; in-service event, injury, disease, duty, or exposure; and a relationship between the current disability and service. Continuity, chronicity, presumptions, combat provisions, and competent lay evidence may affect analysis but do not erase missing elements. [CFR-3.303] [USC-1110] [USC-1154] [CASE-SHEDDEN]

## Evidence character

A person is generally competent to report observable symptoms and events within personal knowledge. Medical diagnosis or causation may require expertise depending on complexity. Lack of contemporaneous medical documentation is relevant but cannot automatically render lay evidence not credible. Credibility remains a fact determination. [CASE-LAYNO] [CASE-JANDREAU] [CASE-BUCHANAN] [CASE-CALUZA]

## Preexisting conditions and aggravation

Analyze entrance findings, presumption of soundness, in-service change, natural progression, and burden rules without assuming the result. Use current statutes, regulations, and qualified review for contested cases. [USC-1111] [USC-1153] [CFR-3.304] [CFR-3.306]

## Guard and Reserve

Identify exact duty status for each event. Active duty, ACDUTRA, and INACDUTRA definitions and disease/injury distinctions can be outcome-determinative. Do not infer qualifying service from membership alone. [USC-101] [CFR-3.6]

## Evidence matrix

For each theory list supporting evidence, missing evidence, contradictions, source provenance, and uncertainty. Never suppress adverse facts; explain how a qualified representative may address conflicts truthfully.
