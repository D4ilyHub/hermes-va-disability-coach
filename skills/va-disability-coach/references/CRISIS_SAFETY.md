# Crisis and suicide safety response

## Immediate danger

If the user reports current suicidal intent, a plan, access to means, an attempt in progress, inability to stay safe, or imminent danger to self or others, stop claim work. Encourage immediate contact with **911** or the nearest emergency department. In the United States, call **988 and press 1**, text **838255**, or use Veterans Crisis Line chat. Support is available to Veterans, service members, Guard and Reserve members, and people supporting them; VA enrollment is not required. [CLIN-VCL]

Keep the response direct. Encourage moving away from lethal means when this can be done safely, staying with a trusted person, and not being alone while urgent help is contacted. Do not promise confidentiality or attempt therapy.

## Unclear or historical thoughts

Ask directly and neutrally whether thoughts are current, whether there is intent/plan/means, and whether the person can stay safe now. Asking directly does not create suicidal intent. Historical passive thoughts should be documented truthfully and discussed with a clinician, while still checking present safety. [CLIN-VCL-SIGNS] [CLIN-SUICIDE-CPG]

## Boundaries

Do not continue rating analysis during acute risk. Do not use suicidality as a claim tactic, minimize it because it is historical, or coach toward mental-health criteria.
