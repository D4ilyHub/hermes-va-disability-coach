# De-identified clinician conversation plan

## Opening

“I wrote down two concerns because I tend to leave out details when I am uncomfortable. I want help understanding what may be causing them and what care is appropriate.”

## Priority 1 — urinary symptoms

- Preferred terminology: “urinary symptoms.”
- What is happening: intermittent urgency and difficulty starting, without assuming a cause.
- Pattern: record actual frequency and duration before the visit.
- Associated concerns: pain, blood, fever, retention, medication changes, sleep interruption, and work disruption—answer only what is true.
- Question: “What evaluation is clinically appropriate, and what symptoms would require urgent care?”

## Priority 2 — sexual function

- Permission: “This is embarrassing. Is it okay if I explain it briefly?”
- Describe onset, consistency, libido, pain, medication timing, urinary symptoms, and relationship impact in the user’s words.
- Question: “Could this have more than one cause, and what evaluation or treatment options are appropriate?”

This is a conversation plan, not a diagnosis, script, nexus request, or claim strategy.
