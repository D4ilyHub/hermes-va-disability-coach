# Contradictions and resolution notes

## Proposed rules versus current eCFR

The 2022 mental and respiratory proposals describe possible changes but are not encoded as current criteria without a final effective rule. Current eCFR controls.

## M21-1 versus statute/regulation

M21-1 may explain agency processing but cannot override controlling statute, regulation, or precedent. Material divergence is surfaced and referred for accredited review.

## Board decisions versus precedent

A Board decision may contain useful reasoning but is generally nonprecedential. The regulation and controlling appellate cases determine general authority.

## Bilateral factor assumptions

Older calculators may always apply the bilateral factor. Current § 4.26(d), effective in 2023, requires the most-favorable exception when exclusion yields a higher combined evaluation. The local engine tests qualifying subsets.

## Rating changes over time

Musculoskeletal, genitourinary, skin, and digestive criteria have effective-date transitions. A current snapshot cannot be applied automatically to an earlier staged period. Historical criteria require separate research.

## BDD timing summaries

Informal sources sometimes describe BDD as “within 180 days” without the 90-day closing point, examination availability, SHA Part A, or exclusions. Current VA guidance controls.
