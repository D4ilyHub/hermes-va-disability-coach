# Known research and product gaps

- The bundled rating snapshot is intentionally partial, not a complete machine-readable Part 4 reproduction.
- Historical rating criteria and effective-date transitions are not automated.
- Hearing tables, TBI facets, complex vision/audiology, muscle groups, dental/oral, skin treatment definitions, digestive changes, and many organ-system codes require manual review.
- M21-1 deep links and internal organization can change without stable public permalinks.
- Public DBQ and form PDFs may change; this release indexes official landing pages rather than parsing every PDF.
- The combined engine cannot determine whether inputs are legally separate, correctly coded, protected, staged, or subject to the amputation/paired-organ rules.
- IDES/MEB/PEB implementation varies by service and facts; only cautious education is provided.
- Guard/Reserve duty status and line-of-duty questions often need record-specific professional review.
- Clinical sources guide communication and safety but cannot diagnose an individual.
- Link availability was not guaranteed for every registry row; maintainers must re-check moving or archived sources.
