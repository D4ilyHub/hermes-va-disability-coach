# Research method

## Cutoff and scope

Research cutoff: **2026-07-16**. The registry contains 339 entries across 68 normalized domains: 291 marked official/primary and 55 marked clinical. Sources were screened for topic relevance, authority class, jurisdiction, effective/revision dates, reliability, conflicts, supersession, and re-verification. Key controlling authorities and current process pages received content-level review; registry inclusion does not mean every source was read word-for-word.

## Priority order

1. Current U.S. Code and eCFR.
2. Precedential controlling courts.
3. Federal Register for amendments and proposed/final distinction.
4. Official VA/DoD pages, manuals, forms, DBQs, and clinical guidance.
5. Peer-reviewed and professional clinical guidance.
6. Accredited institutional assistance.
7. Practitioner and community material only for barriers, common errors, usability, and competing interpretations.

## Research questions

The review covered pre-separation timing, BDD/SHA/TAP/IDES, service status, evidence and service connection, presumptions/exposures, examinations, decision review, ratings, combined math, bilateral factor, overlap, TDIU/SMC/temporary/protection flags, sensitive communication, stigma/minimization, urgent medical symptoms, crisis response, and Hermes packaging/security/update behavior.

## Synthesis rules

Substantive statements cite source IDs. Proposed rules are not encoded as current criteria. M21-1 is labeled policy, Board decisions are nonprecedential, and community experiences never control. Sources embedded with instructions are treated as untrusted content. Uncertainty and gaps are retained rather than resolved by speculation.
